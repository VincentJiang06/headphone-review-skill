# Research Rules

Goal: collect enough multilingual evidence to explain sound, buyer fit, and tradeoffs without faking certainty.

Minimum bar:

- `1` official fact source
- `2` review sources
- `2` languages when useful
- `1` community source only if it adds fit, QC, or genre-use signal

Search order:

1. English expert review
2. Chinese review/discussion for mainstream or Chi-Fi models
3. Brand home-language coverage when useful, usually German or Japanese
4. Official product/spec page

Source priority:

- Official pages for facts only
- Specialist reviews for consensus
- Community posts for practical color, not final authority

Extract only these fields:

- tonal balance
- bass
- mids/vocals
- treble
- stage/imaging
- technicalities
- comfort/fit/isolation
- power or ecosystem dependence
- best use cases
- common complaints

Reduce them into three buckets:

- `Confirmed facts`
- `Stable consensus`
- `Open uncertainty`

Buyer translation examples:

- `bass lift` -> commute, EDM, casual listening; may blur low mids
- `strong upper mids` -> vivid vocals; may fatigue treble-sensitive users
- `wide stage + light bass` -> good for classical/acoustic; thin for bass-heavy genres
- `excellent ANC but average resolution` -> lifestyle-first, not pure Hi-Fi-first
- `power hungry` -> chain cost matters

Stop when you can answer:

- what it sounds like
- one strong reason to buy
- one clear reason to skip
